\subsection{Dataset Examples}
\label{app:examples}

\para{Same-category example} (fine-grained semantic matching):
\begin{quote}
\textbf{Query}: ``What's the forecast for Los Angeles this week?'' \\
\textbf{Tools}: \texttt{[openweathermap\_api, weatherapi\_com, world\_weather\_online, weather\_gov\_api]} \\
\textbf{LLM selection}: \texttt{openweathermap\_api} (position~0)
\end{quote}

\para{Mixed-category example} (coarse routing):
\begin{quote}
\textbf{Query}: ``Compute the integral of $x^3$ from 0 to 5.'' \\
\textbf{Tools}: \texttt{[wolfram\_alpha\_api, openweathermap\_api, google\_search\_api, jupyter\_kernel\_api]} \\
\textbf{LLM selection}: \texttt{wolfram\_alpha\_api} (position~0)
\end{quote}

\para{Adversarial perturbation example}:
\begin{quote}
\textbf{Query}: ``What's the forecast for London this weekend?'' \\
\textbf{Condition}: Adversarial --- \texttt{wolfram\_alpha\_api} carries the weather description \\
\textbf{LLM selection}: \texttt{wolfram\_alpha\_api} (model follows the description, not the name)
\end{quote}

\subsection{Software and Hardware}
\label{app:software}

All experiments ran on a single CPU (no GPU).  LLM experiments took approximately 15 minutes;
probing experiments took approximately 8 minutes.  Key library versions:
sentence-transformers (latest), transformers~5.3.0, scikit-learn~1.8.0, scipy~1.17.1,
openai~2.26.0.
